this time last year
your name on my lips
felt like cool autumn
rain falling on my
bedroom window

the distant evening sun
making shapeshifting
silhouettes on the far
wall that I would have
watched in quiet contentment

if I could have pulled myself
away from the blankets
and sheets that enveloped
our bodies deep in
wordless conversation

the curve of your hips speaking
softly to my fingertips

but I awoke this morning and I
poured a cup of coffee and your
name fell across my lips and for
a moment the taste was
indiscernible as if

it had fallen there
for the first time
and I had mistaken
it for another

 

written November 11th, 2013

She is not a hurricane; not
head-turning world-upending

headline-writing majesty of
global atmospheric import;

not destruction of the most
beautiful sort; not chaos;

not floodwaters that rise and
leave you ragged and aching.

She is the air that blankets
the earth after the storm

has passed; she is the air
and I am breathing deeply.

I miss you in the smallest sense. I shrink
to dust and bones, a beggarman, bereft
without your touch. These empty hands once knew

the comfort that your company can bring,
like rain upon the earth that once had slept
in wait to know once more the vernal bloom.

stiff and stilted
dust the rust off
a return to form must begin with cliches and
stuttering

pregnant pauses will linger
do not dress them up on pedestals with vague
attempts at gleaning substance when
there is nothing to glean

just see to it that they pass along
in due time

A shudder and the rust is falling free—
belated, ever so, but falling still—
towards delicate repose with glacial pace.

A cough, a stutter, a collapsing space
besieged and to be swept, a windowsill;
the settled dust unsettled soon to be.
